Professional Background

I started my professional career with a job at PACCAR, Inc working for the Embedded Engineering department directly out of college. I received an offer to become a software engineer on a Windows application that's purpose was to facilitate building and servicing heavy duty trucks. Our application is a combination of Java and C# and creates a dynamic application that is used globally. During my time as a software engineer, I was tasked with creating algorithms that would perform a number of different tasks through communication with embedded devices. Those embedded devices (Electronic Control Units - ECUs) would then perform actions that would diagnose whether the system was working properly. The other side of what our team handled was the updating of those ECUs.

One of my greatest accomplishments as a software engineer for PACCAR, Inc was my contribution to the launch of the PACCAR 12 speed transmission. I spent many hours learning a new (to me) protocol for CAN (J1939) that was used to communicate with the transmission controller. This required learning a new skill in that there was a lot of time debugging wiring issues. On the software side, we were tasked with trying to trim down the time it took to update the ECU from 3 minutes down to 1.5. While I was successful in optomizing the solution from 3 minutes to a stable 2 minutes, this still was too long for our manufacturing plants. As a result, we were tasked with creating a new station strictly for programming the transmission controller. I was in charge of analyzing the requirements from the manufacturing engineer, and creating a design that would satisfy all stakeholders.
